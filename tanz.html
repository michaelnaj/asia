Alex

This is my friend, Alex. When I told Alex the nature of my project he informed me that he did not grow up in the South and that his only real experience with living in the South has come from his time at Duke. He said that he would like to quickly comment on the fact that, after coming to the South, he began to see racial divides unlike ever before. Alex is very social, yet he wanted to draw attention to the disturbing nature of the social life at Duke -- namely that it is so segregated. The idea that social institutions, such as fraternities, sororities, and selected living groups, are often demarcated by race and colour is ludicrous. All peoples are capable of interacting with one another, but segregation hinders this process. Alex wanted his contribution to my project to urge the people of Duke to transgress prescribed boundaries based on origin.
